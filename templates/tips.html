Food Freshness



Tips for Preserving your Food

Food Groups

  1. *Prepare Meat Before Freezing*

    • -Air is very damaging to the meat overtime since it can cause frezerburn
    • -Since air is damaging for the meat, it is advisable to over-wrap the packaging to make sure no air can enter the package (with plastic bags and heavy-duty aluminum foil)
    • -The use of a vacuum sealer can be a huge help in removing the air from the packaging.
    • -Use air-tight containers such as plastic or freezer-safe jars and cans.
    • -Use wraps such as heavyweight aluminum foil, plastic freezer bags or polyethylene wrap and bags.
    • -Remove as many bones as possible before freezing as bones take up space and can contribute to freezer burn.
    • -Place freezer paper or wrap between slices or patties of meat to make separation easier after freezing

  2. *Know how long you can safely store frozen meat*

    • -Uncooked meat (such as steaks or chops) can be kept frozen safely for 4-12 months.
    • -Uncooked ground meat can only be kept safely for 3-4 months.
    • -Cooked meat can be stored for 2-3 months.
    • -Hotdogs, ham, and lunch meats can be frozen for 1-2 months.
    • -Poultry (uncooked turkey and chicken, as well as cooked turkey and chicken) can be kept for 3-12 months.
    • -Wild game can be kept for 8-12 months.
    • -Make sure to maintain a freezer or cooler temperature of 0 °F (−18 °C) or below.

  3. *Be sure to label all containers and packages*

    • -The label should contain the type of meat (chicken breast, steak, ground beef, etc.), whether it is raw or cooked, and the date it was frozen.
    • -To help you find things later, it is a good idea to group like items together. For example, put all the chicken together, all the beef together, all the pork together.
    • -Use the oldest items first to avoid having to discard out of date or freezer-burned food.

  4. *Use an electric freezer to store meat*

    • -You can use the freezer compartment that is attached to your refrigerator or use a stand-alone freezer.
    • -Stand-alone freezers are larger than the compartment on a refrigerator.
    • -Be aware that a freezer does use electricity so your energy bills will be higher if you use a stand-alone freezer in addition to your refrigerator. The cost increase will depend on the size of the freezer and how energy-efficient the model is.

  1. *Store Fresh Fish on Ice in the Refrigerator*

    • -Fish rots quickly, even in the fridge, unless it is iced. Fish usually swim in water that is colder than air, sometimes a lot colder.
    • -Use a cooling rack that fits inside a large shallow container such as a roasting pan. The cooling rack should be a grid or mesh design with many openings. If the rack doesn't have legs, find a way to elevate it inside the large container.

  2. *Freezing Fresh Fish*

    1. -Remove the fish from the store packaging.
    2. -Rinse it under cold water and use paper towels to dry it.
    3. -Put the fish in freezer bags or containers.
    4. -Label and date the bags or containers.
    5. -Put in a freezer set at zero degrees or colder.

    • *For the best taste and nutrition, thaw and prepare the fresh fish within two weeks. Thaw the fish in the coldest part of the refrigerator.*

  1. *Keep uncooked poultry cold prior to cooking it*

    • -If you plan on purchasing poultry, make sure the grocery store is your last stop of the day before heading home.
    • -If it's warm outside, carry an insulated bag or cooler to place the poultry in on your way home from the store.

  2. *Prevent cross-contamination by storing poultry separately*

    • -Ask that your poultry be wrapped in a plastic bag to prevent leakage that could cross-contaminate other vegetables, fruit, or meat you've purchased.
    • -This also applies when you put the poultry in your refrigerator when you get home.

  3. *Store poultry on the lowest shelf in the fridge*

    • -When you put it in the fridge there's a strong chance some of that moisture will run off the poultry and onto other items in the fridge, contaminating them with harmful bacteria.
    • -If you store poultry in the fridge for longer than 3 days, not only will it not taste as good when it's cooked, but it will become a safety hazard.

  4. *Refrigerate poultry for 3 days maximum*

    • -Poultry is generally meant to be eaten as soon as possible.
    • -This also applies when you put the poultry in your refrigerator when you get home.
